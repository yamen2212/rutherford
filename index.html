About Rutherford

Ernest Rutherford was a New Zealand-born physicist who became known as the father of nuclear physics. He is best known for his discovery of the atomic nucleus.

The Experiment

The Rutherford experiment, also known as the gold foil experiment, was conducted in 1909 by Hans Geiger and Ernest Marsden under the direction of Ernest Rutherford.
